<h3>The Reserve Army of Labor:&nbsp;Dual Labor Markets and Firm Dynamics</h3>

<p><em class="presentations">Presentations: Macro Workshop, PSE (May&nbsp;2024); Macro Reading Group, University of Edinburgh (April&nbsp;2025)</em></p>


<!-- Collapsible abstract -->

<div class="abstract-block">

  <input type="checkbox" id="abs-ral">

  <label for="abs-ral">Abstract&nbsp;</label>

  <div class="abs-text">

    <p>I develop a heterogenous-firm model with decreasing returns to scale, in the spirit of Elsby and Gottfries (2021), extended to dual labour types and unionised wage bargaining. The firms can hire through two channels. First, a formal labour market subject to search frictions, hiring and firing costs, and unionised wages. Second, an informal labour market where labour contractors supply workers for a per-head fee without frictional adjustment. Formal labour adjustment costs generate a canonical inaction region as in Bentolila and Bertola (1990), where firms find it optimal to keep formal employment unchanged. However, frictionless informal hiring allows firms to absorb productivity shocks, leading to decline in labour misallocation. On the other hand, firms strategicially ‘overemploy’ informal workers beyond the production optimum –  generating downward wage pressure for firm-union negotiated formal wages. This is mainly afforded by larger firms can pay high contracting fee, contributing to aggregate labour misallocation. The model replicates key empirical patterns in India’s registered manufacturing sector, where 'contract' workers act as a substitutable ‘reserve workforce’ - providing flexibility and surplus value of formal wage reduction. </p>

  </div>
